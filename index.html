About Me

- Coding never crossed my mind however I was always curious about technology and how things work. I remember when my dad bought our first PC in 1998. It just had the MS-DOS operation system on it. I taught myself how to send and receive emails as I wanted to chat with my friends. Once I set my mind to solving a particular problem I am very motivated, persistent, resourceful and creative in finding solutions.

I have NCFE CACHE Level2 Certificate in Understanding working in the Health Sector, a Bachelor Degree in “Business administration and management” and few other certificates in coaching, time planning and leadership.

I have been self-employed for the last 10 years working in cosmetic sales. I prepared and delivered presentations, created motivational programs for my team, and ran structured training sessions.

My husband has worked closely with software developers in the past and always believed that I would be great at coding. He saw that I like to solve problems, am very patient and methodical in searching for solutions but I always resisted the idea as I thought that you need to be exceptional at maths and computer science for coding.

I found out about “Founders and Coders” from a housemate who’s the head of HR in a Health-Tech NGO. She mainly recruits software developers and has hired a few graduates from “founders and coders”. She told me about your bootcamps and your different approach to teaching. When I researched FAC I found an incredible community of inspirational people, hungry for knowledge to change their lives and potentially change the world!

My journey in coding started in March 2020 after I decided to apply for a summer cohort. I started to solve the prerequisite tasks on the “freeCodeCamp” site. I was so engrossed in the exercises that I spent days and days just implementing small missions. It really hooked me in. It was such a great feeling to create something, to learn how everything works and to solve problems.

I’ve run through many of the tasks on the FCC page multiple times now. The first time everything was very new, exciting and unfamiliar to me. After I put all my knowledge into practise by creating a web page and solving Kata’s, I ran through the tasks a second time, everything made even more sense and really crystallised in my mind. It was like a small revelation!

I’m very passionate about travel, baking, reading, phycology, self-learning, and gardening

For me coding is like baking a cake. You need to follow very specific instructions; however there’s a level of flexibility and creativity to express yourself in very unique ways.

-Paulo Coelho-

My Why

- I am always interested in learning new skills and am energised by the prospect of diving into an exciting new long term career. I think “Founders and Coders” is a fantastic way to do this. I like the idea of FAC as a community of like-minded people and I would love to be a part of it. I know that I can learn a lot from others and believe that I have a lot to give back.
