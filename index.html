About Me

Coding never crossed my mind however, I was always curious about technology and how things work. In 1998, my dad bought our first PC. On it, there only existed the MS-DOS operation system. Using our PC, I taught myself how to send and receive emails as I wanted to chat with my friends. Once I set my mind to solving a particular problem, I am very motivated, persistent, resourceful and creative in finding solutions.

I have NCFE CACHE Level2 Certificate in Understanding Working in the Health Sector, a Bachelor Degree in Business Administration and Management and few other certificates in coaching, time planning and leadership.

I have been self-employed for the last 10 years working in cosmetic sales. During this time, I prepared and delivered presentations, created motivational programs for my team, and ran structured training sessions.

My partner has worked closely with software developers in the past and always believed that I would be great at coding. He recognised my ability to solve problems with patience and search for solutions methodically. However, I always resisted the idea as I presumed that coding required exceptional skills in maths and computer science.

I found out about “Founders and Coders” from a housemate who’s the Head of HR in a Health-Tech NGO. She mainly recruits software developers and has hired a few graduates from “founders and coders”. She made me aware of your bootcamps and your different approach to teaching. When I researched FAC I found an incredible community of inspirational people, hungry for knowledge. I could see their motivation to change their lives and potentially change the world!

My journey in coding started in March 2020 after I decided to apply for a summer cohort. I started to solve the prerequisite tasks on the “freeCodeCamp” site. I was so engrossed in the exercises that I spent days and days just implementing small missions. It really grasped my attention. It was such a great feeling to create something, to learn how everything works and to solve problems.

I’ve run through many of the tasks on the FCC page, multiple times. The first time everything was very new, exciting and unfamiliar to me. After I put all my knowledge into practise by creating a web page and solving Kata’s, I ran through the tasks a second time. Everything became even more clear and really crystallised in my mind. It was like a small revelation!

I’m very passionate about travel, baking, reading, psychology, self-learning, and gardening.
Speak: English, Lithuanian, Russian, German languages and now I’m learning Arabic language.

For me coding is like baking a cake. You need to follow very specific instructions; however, there’s a level of flexibility and creativity to express yourself in very unique ways.

My Why

This is third time I have tried to get onto this course. The first time was for the 2020 summer cohort. As soon as I found out about the possibility of joining this community, I started trying to fulfil all the necessary prerequisites. After a while I realised that I didn’t have enough time to finish everything, absorb all information and be ready to apply, so I decided to give myself some extra time to digest everything and wait until the winter cohort.

Before the winter application opening, I had almost finished all my prerequisites. I then learned, suddenly, that the winter cohort had been cancelled. I would be lying if I was to say that I didn’t feel sad. Of course, I did, but life didn’t stop, and I still had hope. I didn’t stop learning and now I'm applying for this spring cohort. I really hope that the third time I’ll be lucky, and I will be accepted onto this course. Fingers crossed.

I came to this country 2 years ago; not to find a better life, but to be with my partner. I left my family, close friends and a job that I loved, with the hope to start a new life here. When I arrived, I felt like a small girl in the big, big world: no friends, no education (my degree here had no value and I didn't want to work in management) and virtually no language. I had to start everything from scratch, and I lacked confidence. I understood that I had two choices: sit at home and cry or do something about it. I decided to move forward. I learned the language, finished a social care course with the hope to start my first job in my new home, but COVID-19 changed all my plans. So, I needed to find another way to realise myself, to create, and be useful to society.

Deep inside I always wanted to be part of a community, to do something important. It was by accident that I was introduced to programing, but I truly believe that there are no real accidents in life. Everything comes at the right time.

So, now I'm writing to you through my own built website (that I’m very proud of), with a big will to go forward, to dive deeper in the ocean of knowledge. I know that here in FAC I will find (and have already found) an incredible community of amazing people and I know that together we can create something mind-blowing what will change the world.
